## Leave Types

### 1. Annual Leave / Vacation Leave

**Purpose**: Paid time off for rest, recreation, and personal matters.

**Entitlement**:
- New employees: Prorated based on start date
- Standard allocation: 20 days per year
- Senior employees (5+ years): 25 days per year
- Management level: 30 days per year

**Rules**:
- Minimum request: 1 day
- Maximum continuous: 15 days (longer requires special approval)
- Advance notice: Minimum 2 weeks for requests over 5 days
- Carryover: Maximum 5 days to next year
- Expiry: Unused days expire after 12 months (except carried over days)

**Documentation**: Not required

**Approval Process**: Manager approval required

### 2. Sick Leave

**Purpose**: Time off for illness, injury, or medical appointments.

**Entitlement**:
- 12 days per year
- Does not accumulate year to year
- Resets annually

**Rules**:
- Can be requested retroactively with documentation
- Medical certificate required for:
  - Absences exceeding 3 consecutive days
  - Frequent short-term absences (pattern-based)
- Emergency sick leave: Notify manager immediately
- No advance approval needed for emergencies

**Documentation**: 
- Medical certificate for 3+ consecutive days
- Doctor's note for medical appointments

**Approval Process**: 
- Manager notification required
- HR review for extended absences

### 3. Personal Leave

**Purpose**: Time off for personal matters, family responsibilities, or emergencies.

**Entitlement**:
- 5 days per year
- Non-cumulative

**Rules**:
- Short notice acceptable (minimum 24 hours if possible)
- Subject to manager approval
- Must not conflict with critical business periods
- Reason required but details optional

**Documentation**: Not required unless specifically requested

**Approval Process**: Manager approval required

### 4. Maternity Leave

**Purpose**: Time off for childbirth and postnatal care.

**Entitlement**:
- 16 weeks paid leave
- Can start up to 4 weeks before due date
- Minimum 6 weeks post-delivery

**Rules**:
- Must notify HR at least 4 weeks before intended start date
- Medical certificate from healthcare provider required
- Flexible return-to-work options available
- Option to extend with unpaid leave

**Documentation**:
- Medical certificate confirming pregnancy and due date
- Birth certificate (for benefits processing)

**Approval Process**: HR approval (automatic for eligible employees)

### 5. Paternity Leave

**Purpose**: Time off for fathers/partners following birth or adoption.

**Entitlement**:
- 2 weeks paid leave
- Must be taken within 8 weeks of birth/adoption

**Rules**:
- Notify manager at least 2 weeks in advance
- Flexible scheduling (can be taken in blocks)
- Non-transferable

**Documentation**:
- Birth certificate or adoption papers

**Approval Process**: Manager and HR approval

### 6. Parental Leave

**Purpose**: Extended leave for child care (beyond maternity/paternity).

**Entitlement**:
- Up to 12 weeks unpaid leave
- Available to both parents
- Must be taken within first year of birth/adoption

**Rules**:
- Request at least 6 weeks in advance
- Can be taken in blocks (minimum 1 week)
- Job protection guaranteed
- Benefits continue during leave (per policy)

**Documentation**:
- Formal written request
- Birth certificate or adoption papers

**Approval Process**: HR approval required

### 7. Compassionate Leave / Bereavement Leave

**Purpose**: Time off following death of family member or close friend.

**Entitlement**:
- Immediate family: Up to 5 days paid
- Extended family: Up to 3 days paid
- Additional unpaid leave available if needed

**Immediate Family Includes**:
- Spouse/partner
- Children
- Parents
- Siblings
- Grandparents
- In-laws (parents and siblings of spouse)

**Rules**:
- Immediate notification to manager
- Additional unpaid days can be requested
- Travel time may be granted for distant locations

**Documentation**:
- Death certificate or funeral notice
- May be required for extended absences

**Approval Process**: Manager notification; HR for extended periods

### 8. Study Leave / Educational Leave

**Purpose**: Time off for professional development and education.

**Entitlement**:
- Up to 10 days per year (for approved courses)
- Subject to business needs

**Rules**:
- Must be job-related or career development
- Pre-approval required from manager and HR
- May require cost-sharing agreement
- Examination days included

**Documentation**:
- Course details and schedule
- Proof of enrollment
- Certificate of completion (for records)

**Approval Process**: Manager and HR approval required

### 9. Unpaid Leave

**Purpose**: Extended time off without pay for personal reasons.

**Entitlement**:
- Up to 3 months per request
- Subject to approval

**Rules**:
- Request at least 8 weeks in advance
- Business impact assessment required
- Job protection not guaranteed beyond 3 months
- Benefits may be affected (check with HR)
- Must have exhausted paid leave options

**Documentation**:
- Formal written request with detailed reason

**Approval Process**: Manager and HR approval; may require executive approval

### 10. Public Holidays

**Entitlement**:
- All official public holidays
- Variable by location/country

**Rules**:
- Automatically granted (no request needed)
- If required to work: Compensatory time off or overtime pay
- Does not count against leave balance

**Common Public Holidays**:
- New Year's Day
- National/Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Religious holidays (as applicable)
- Year-end holidays

### 11. Sabbatical Leave

**Purpose**: Extended break for long-serving employees.

**Entitlement**:
- Eligible after 5 years of continuous service
- Duration: Up to 3 months
- Frequency: Once every 5 years

**Rules**:
- Plan at least 6 months in advance
- Detailed knowledge transfer required
- Partial or full pay (per organization policy)
- Return-to-work guarantee

**Documentation**:
- Formal application with purpose
- Knowledge transfer plan

**Approval Process**: Senior management approval required

## Leave Policies

### General Leave Rules

1. **Minimum Notice Periods**:
   - 1-2 days leave: 3 days notice
   - 3-5 days leave: 1 week notice
   - 6+ days leave: 2 weeks notice
   - Exception: Emergency/sick leave

2. **Maximum Leave Duration**:
   - Standard: 15 consecutive days
   - Extended: Requires special approval
   - Sabbatical: Up to 3 months

3. **Blackout Periods**:
   - Peak business seasons (defined by department)
   - Critical project phases
   - End of fiscal year (finance department)
   - Limited number of team members on leave simultaneously

4. **Half-Day Leave**:
   - Counts as 0.5 days
   - Morning: Start to 1 PM
   - Afternoon: 1 PM to end of day
   - Minimum 4 hours

### Leave Request Procedures

1. **Submission**:
   - Submit through Employee Leave Tracker system
   - Include all required information
   - Attach supporting documents when required

2. **Approval Hierarchy**:
   - Level 1: Direct Manager
   - Level 2: Department Head (for extended leave)
   - Level 3: HR (for special leave types)
   - Level 4: Executive (for exceptional cases)

3. **Response Time**:
   - Managers should respond within 3 business days
   - Emergency requests: Within 24 hours
   - HR reviews: Within 5 business days

4. **Notification**:
   - Automatic email notification on status change
   - In-app notifications
   - SMS for urgent updates (if enabled)

### Leave Balance Management

1. **Accrual**:
   - Annual leave accrues monthly
   - Prorated for new hires and departing employees
   - Starts from employment date

2. **Carryover**:
   - Maximum 5 days annual leave to next year
   - Must be used within Q1 of new year
   - Sick leave does not carry over

3. **Payout on Termination**:
   - Unused annual leave paid out
   - Calculation: (Daily rate × Unused days)
   - Processed with final paycheck

4. **Negative Balance**:
   - Generally not permitted
   - Exceptions require executive approval
   - Recovery plan required if approved

## Accrual Rules

### Standard Accrual Calculation

**Annual Leave**:
```
Monthly Accrual = Annual Entitlement / 12
Example: 20 days per year = 1.67 days per month
```

**Proration for New Hires**:
```
Prorated Amount = (Annual Entitlement / 12) × Months Remaining in Year
Example: Started July 1st = (20 / 12) × 6 = 10 days
```

**Proration for Departing Employees**:
```
Earned Amount = (Annual Entitlement / 12) × Months Worked
Balance = Earned - Used
```

### Accrual Schedules

1. **Immediate Accrual**:
   - Leave balance available immediately
   - Used by startups and small companies

2. **Monthly Accrual**:
   - Most common method
   - Accrues on first day of each month
   - Based on active employment days

3. **Annual Grant**:
   - Full year allocation granted on anniversary
   - Or on January 1st for calendar year basis

4. **Milestone-Based**:
   - Increases with tenure
   - Example:
     - 0-2 years: 15 days
     - 3-5 years: 20 days
     - 6+ years: 25 days

## Approval Guidelines

### For Managers

**Approval Criteria**:
1. ✅ Adequate team coverage during absence
2. ✅ No conflict with critical deadlines
3. ✅ Employee has sufficient balance
4. ✅ Reasonable advance notice provided
5. ✅ Required documentation submitted

**Rejection Guidelines**:
- Provide clear, specific reason
- Suggest alternative dates if possible
- Document decision
- Remain professional and empathetic

**Considerations**:
- Business continuity
- Team morale and fairness
- Employee's leave history
- Personal circumstances
- Legal obligations

### For HR Administrators

**Review Checklist**:
- Policy compliance
- Documentation completeness
- Balance availability
- Legal requirements
- Special approvals needed
- System accuracy

**Intervention Scenarios**:
- Policy violations
- Extended absences
- Pattern of abuse
- Legal compliance issues
- Executive escalations

## Special Circumstances

### Remote Work Arrangements

- Leave policies apply equally to remote workers
- Time zone considerations for half-day leave
- Documentation via digital means acceptable

### Part-Time Employees

- Prorated leave entitlement based on working hours
- Example: 50% time = 50% of full-time entitlement
- Accrual calculated proportionally

### Contract Workers

- Leave entitlement per contract terms
- May differ from permanent employees
- Consult contract and HR for specifics

### Extended Medical Leave

- Beyond standard sick leave
- Requires medical certification
- May transition to disability benefits
- Job protection per local laws
- Return-to-work planning

### Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) - US

- Up to 12 weeks unpaid leave
- Job protection guaranteed
- Qualifying reasons:
  - Birth/adoption of child
  - Serious health condition
  - Care for family member with serious health condition
  - Military family leave

### Emergency Leave

- Immediate approval process
- Retroactive documentation accepted
- Includes:
  - Natural disasters
  - Family emergencies
  - Sudden illness
  - Critical personal situations

## Compliance and Legal

### Legal Requirements

Organizations must comply with:
- Local labor laws
- National employment standards
- Industry-specific regulations
- Union agreements (if applicable)

### Data Privacy

- Leave records treated as confidential
- Access restricted to authorized personnel
- Retention per legal requirements
- GDPR/privacy law compliance

### Audit and Reporting

- Regular policy audits
- Compliance reporting
- Usage statistics
- Pattern analysis
- Legal documentation

### Anti-Discrimination

- Fair and consistent policy application
- No discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Reasonable accommodations for disabilities
- Equal treatment across all employee groups

### Documentation and Record Keeping

Required records:
- Leave requests and approvals
- Medical certificates
- Balance statements
- Policy acknowledgments
- Audit trails

Retention period: Minimum 7 years (or per local law)

- Employee feedback incorporated
- Management approval required

### Communication

- All employees notified of changes
- Training provided for significant updates
- Documentation always accessible
- FAQ section maintained

### Policy Acknowledgment

- Tracked in system
- Required annually or upon updates
